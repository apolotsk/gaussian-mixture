### Gaussian Mixture

Description:
- They have $n$ Gaussians.
- They select one Gaussian according to some distribution.
- They sample from the selected Gaussian.
- They repeat the process several times.
- They tell us a set of samples.
- They do not tell us which Gaussian was used for each sample.
- They do not tell us the distribution to select a Gaussian.

Definitions:
- $x$ is the sample value.
  - $x$ is the _observable_ data.
    ![Histogram of the observable data](.Gaussian%20Mixture.md/Histogram%20of%20the%20observable%20data.svg)
- $z$ is the selected Gaussian.
  - $z$ is the _latent_ data.
  - $z \in \{ 1, 2, ..., n \}$.
    ![Histogram of the observable data and the latent data](.Gaussian%20Mixture.md/Histogram%20of%20the%20observable%20data%20and%20the%20latent%20data.svg)
- That is, they know the set of $(x,z)$, but tell us only the set of $x$.
- We want to infer the biases (the parameters).
  - This allows to infer the set of $(x,z)$.
- We use the _Expectation-maximization_ algorithm given the set of $x$.

$Œ∏ := \{ p(z|Œ∏), \mu_{z|Œ∏}, \sigma_{z|Œ∏} \text{ for } z \in \{1, 2, ..., n \} \}$
- $p(z|Œ∏)$ is the probability of selecting Gaussian $z$.
- $\mu_{z|Œ∏}$ is the mean of the Gaussian $z$.
- $\sigma_{z|Œ∏}$ is the standard deviation of the Gaussian $z$.

$\color{green}{p(x,z|Œ∏)} = p(z|Œ∏) p(x|z,Œ∏)$
- $p(x|z,Œ∏) := \mathcal{N}(x|\mu,\sigma)$
  - $\mathcal{N}(x|\mu,\sigma) := \frac 1 {\sigma {\sqrt {2\pi }}} e^{- \frac {(x-\mu)^2} {2 \sigma^2}}$
  - $\mathcal{N}(x|\mu,\sigma)$ is a normal (Gaussian) distribution.
  - $p(x=a|z,Œ∏)$ is the probability to sample value $a$ from the Gaussian $z$.

$Œ∏_{t+1} := \{ p(z|Œ∏_{t+1}), \mu_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}}, \sigma_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}} \text{ for } z \in \{1, 2, ..., n \} \}$
- $p(z|Œ∏_{t+1}) := \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ |X|$
  - $\sum_x p(z|x,Œ∏_t)$ is the number of coin $z$ choices.
  - $|X|$ is the number of any coin choices.
- $\mu_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}} := \sum_x x \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
- $\sigma_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}} := \sqrt {\sum_x (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}})^2 \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}}$

Proof:
- $p(z|Œ∏_{t+1})$
  - $:= \arg\max_{p(z|Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X)$
  - $\frac \partial {\partial p(z|Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) = 0$
    - $= \frac \partial {\partial p(z|Œ∏)}( L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) - ùúÜ \cdot (\sum_z p(z|Œ∏)-1))$
      - $ùúÜ$ is the _Lagrange multiplier_.
    - $= \frac \partial {\partial p(z|Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) - \frac \partial {\partial p(z|Œ∏)} ùúÜ \cdot (\sum_z p(z|Œ∏)-1)$
    - $= \frac \partial {\partial p(z|Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) - ùúÜ$
    - $= \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}/p(z|Œ∏) - ùúÜ$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}/p(z|Œ∏) - ùúÜ = 0$
  - $ùúÜ = \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}/p(z|Œ∏)$
  - $ùúÜ \cdot p(z|Œ∏) = \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $\sum_z ùúÜ \cdot p(z|Œ∏) = \sum_z \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $ùúÜ \cdot \sum_z p(z|Œ∏) = \sum_x \sum_z \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $ùúÜ \cdot 1 = \sum_x 1$
  - $ùúÜ = |X|$
  - $= \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ |X|$
- $\mu_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}}$
  - $:= \arg\max_{\mu_{z|Œ∏}} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X)$
  - $\frac \partial {\partial \mu_{z|Œ∏}} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) = 0$
    - $= \frac \partial {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) \cdot \frac {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} {\partial \mu_{z|Œ∏}}$
  - $\frac \partial {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X)$
    - $= \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} / p(x|z,Œ∏)$
  - $\frac {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} {\partial \mu_{z|Œ∏}}$
    - $= \frac 1 {\sigma_{z|Œ∏} {\sqrt {2\pi }}} e^{- \frac {(x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2} {2 \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2}} \cdot (-\frac 1 {2\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2} \cdot 2 \cdot (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏}) \cdot (-1))$
    - $= p(x|z,Œ∏) \cdot (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})/\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})/\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 = 0$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏}) = 0$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot \mu_{z|Œ∏} = \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot x$
  - $\mu_{z|Œ∏} \cdot \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} = \sum_x x \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $\mu_{z|Œ∏} = \sum_x x \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $= \sum_x x \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
- $\sigma_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}}$
  - $:= \arg\max_{\sigma_{z|Œ∏}} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X)$
  - $\frac \partial {\partial \sigma_{z|Œ∏}} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) = 0$
  - $= \frac \partial {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} L(Œ∏|Œ∏_t,X) \cdot \frac {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} {\partial \sigma_{z|Œ∏}}$
  - $\frac {\partial p(x|z,Œ∏)} {\partial \sigma_{z|Œ∏}}$
    - $= \frac 1 {\sigma_{z|Œ∏} {\sqrt {2\pi }}} e^{- \frac {(x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2} {2 \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2}} \cdot \frac {-(x-\mu)^2} 2 \frac {-2} {\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^3} - \frac 1 {\sigma_{z|Œ∏} {\sqrt {2\pi }}} e^{- \frac {(x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2} {2 \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2}} \cdot \frac 1 \sigma_{z|Œ∏}$
    - $= p(x|z,Œ∏) \cdot (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^{-3} - p(x|z,Œ∏) \cdot \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^{-1}$
    - $= p(x|z,Œ∏) \cdot \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^{-3} \left( (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2 - \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 \right)$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^{-3} \left( (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2 - \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 \right) = 0$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot \left( (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2 - \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 \right) = 0$
  - $\sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot \sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 = \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \cdot (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2$
  - $\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 \cdot \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} = \sum_x (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2 \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $\sigma_{z|Œ∏}^2 = \sum_x (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏})^2 \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}$
  - $= \sqrt {\sum_x (x-\mu_{z|Œ∏_{t+1}})^2 \cdot \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)} \Big/ \sum_x \color{orange}{p(z|x,Œ∏_t)}}$
